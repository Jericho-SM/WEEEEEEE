Cosmic Phenomenon

DARK ENERGY

Dark energy is the mysterious force causing the universe to expand at an accelerating rate. Unlike matter, it does not clump or emit light, making it invisible yet dominating the cosmos.

What is Dark Energy?

Dark energy is an unknown form of energy that fills space and exerts a repulsive force, driving galaxies apart at increasing speeds. It is estimated to make up about 68% of the universe's total energy content, dwarfing ordinary and dark matter combined.

Scientists infer its existence from the accelerating expansion of the universe observed through supernovae and galaxy surveys. Its true nature remains one of the greatest mysteries in cosmology.

Related Concepts

1. Accelerating Expansion

Observations of distant supernovae show galaxies moving away faster than expected. Dark energy is believed to be the cause, acting as a counterforce to gravity on cosmic scales.

2. Cosmological Constant (Λ)

Proposed by Einstein, the cosmological constant is a simple explanation for dark energy: a constant energy density filling space uniformly. This model fits well with current observations.

3. Quintessence

Some theories suggest dark energy might vary over time, represented by a dynamic field called quintessence. This could explain subtle differences in the expansion rate across cosmic history.

4. Fate of the Universe

The influence of dark energy affects predictions of the universe's future. If it remains constant or strengthens, galaxies will drift farther apart, stars will burn out, and the universe may end in a “Big Freeze.”
