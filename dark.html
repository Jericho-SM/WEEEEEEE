DARK MATTER

Dark matter is the invisible mass holding galaxies together. It cannot be seen, but its gravitational force shapes the universe and keeps cosmic structures from breaking apart.

What is Dark Matter?

Dark matter is a mysterious form of matter that does not emit, absorb, or reflect light, making it completely invisible. Scientists detect it only through its gravitational effects on galaxies and cosmic structures.

It makes up about 27% of the universe, while ordinary matter accounts for only around 5%. The rest is believed to be dark energy.

Without dark matter, galaxies would not stay together. Stars at their outer edges move so fast that they should escape, but instead they remain bound due to the unseen pull of dark matter.

Related Concepts

1. Galaxy Rotation Problem

Observations show that stars far from the center of galaxies move just as fast as those near the center. This contradicts expected physics unless there is extra unseen mass.

This missing mass is what scientists call dark matter.

2. Gravitational Lensing

Massive objects bend light traveling through space. When scientists observe more bending than expected, it reveals hidden mass.

This provides strong evidence for dark matter surrounding galaxies.

3. The Cosmic Web

Galaxies are arranged in a massive network called the cosmic web. These structures are shaped by dark matter, forming the backbone of the universe.

Without dark matter, this large-scale structure would not exist.

4. What is Dark Matter Made Of?

Scientists still do not know what dark matter is made of. Some theories suggest unknown particles, while others propose new physics.

It remains one of the greatest mysteries in modern science.
